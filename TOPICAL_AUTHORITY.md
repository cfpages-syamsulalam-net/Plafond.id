# Plafond.id topical-authority map

Last audited: 2026-07-24 (Asia/Jakarta)

## Role and repository evidence

`Plafond.id` should be an Indonesian ceiling-system knowledge hub: helping owners, designers, contractors, and operators choose, coordinate, install, inspect, and maintain ceiling assemblies. It may explain products commercially, but articles must not duplicate quotation/availability intent or pretend that a panel name alone determines structural, acoustic, fire, moisture, or hygiene performance.

- Canonical repository: `cfpages-syamsulalam-net/Plafond.id`, `main`.
- 4,618 tracked files and 4,035 HTML routes.
- Eight root-level geographic families contain exactly 489 pages each: acoustic, gypsum, GRC, Kalsiboard, wood, metal, PVC, and plywood (3,912 pages).
- Meaningful system/design routes include `/akustik/`, `/coffered-ceiling/`, `/datar/`, `/gipsum/`, `/grc/`, `/kalsiboard/`, `/kayu/`, `/metal/`, `/pvc/`, `/rangka-atap-terekspos/`, `/transparan/`, `/tray-ceiling/`, and `/triplek/`.
- `berita/*`, author/category pagination, WordPress assets, and archive exports are not independent topical coverage.
- The previously observed live failure remains a deployment-evidence gate; repository presence does not prove current public parity.

## Publishing boundaries

- No city, district, province, island, or region-swapped article briefs.
- No universal span, hanger spacing, load, anchor capacity, deflection, fire rating, acoustic rating, moisture resistance, emissions, service life, or price claim.
- An assembly—not a marketing material name—owns structural, acoustic, fire, moisture, and access performance.
- Current Indonesian regulations/standards, exact manufacturer data, tested assembly scope, surveyed site conditions, and responsible specialists govern project values.
- Keep or consolidate legacy/location pages only after GSC, backlink, lead, deployment, and canonical review.

## Cannibalization and internal-link rules

- PLF-01 owns the neutral primer. PLF-02 owns selection inputs. PLF-03 owns material comparison; individual materials must not repeat the comparison.
- PLF-04 owns load path/fixings; PLF-08 owns service coordination; PLF-09 owns site execution.
- PLF-05 owns acoustic outcomes; PLF-06 owns fire/moisture/hygiene exposure; neither may infer assembly performance from a panel name.
- PLF-07 owns form/appearance. PLF-10 owns post-handover symptoms and interventions.
- PLF-11 owns commercial comparison; existing product routes own quotation and availability.
- PLF-12 is the evidence and safety gate, not a duplicate of technical owners.

Publish the twelve P0 briefs first only after deployed-route and legacy reconciliation. Measure specification completeness, qualified leads, assisted conversions, and avoided defects—not page count.
